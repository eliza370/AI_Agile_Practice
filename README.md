# AI_Agile_Practice

This repository is a working toolkit for agile coaches and Scrum Masters who want to use AI as a diagnostic instrument — not a shortcut. The materials here treat AI as a way to surface patterns in real team data that would otherwise take hours to notice, or never get noticed at all.

The underlying philosophy is simple: AI amplifies human judgment. It doesn't replace the coach.

---

## What This Is

A practitioner's working library of AI-assisted agile tools, built and used in real team contexts.

The current focus is on **pattern detection across team artifacts** — the kind of analysis that becomes possible when you stop reading sprint exports one at a time and start asking what the last six sprints are actually telling you.

---

## What's Here

### `/prompts`
Prompt templates organized by ceremony and use case. Each prompt includes context on when to use it, what input it expects, and what you're actually trying to learn. These are not generic starters — they are scoped to specific diagnostic questions.

### `/skills`
Structured AI skill files (markdown-based) that can analyze team artifacts at depth:

- **Refinement session analyzer** — takes a transcript of a refinement session and identifies topics that consumed disproportionate time, recurring points of confusion, and signals that a story isn't ready
- **Sprint pattern detector** — analyzes multiple sprint exports from Jira to surface velocity trends, recurring blockers, and systemic issues that don't show up in any single retrospective
- **Retro pattern analyzer** — works across multiple retrospective frames from Mural or Miro to identify themes that keep resurfacing, even when the team words them differently each time

### `/templates`
Facilitation templates and ceremony canvases that incorporate AI-assisted steps without replacing the human judgment calls that only a coach can make.

---

## What This Is Not

This is not a collection of prompts for generating agile advice.

Asking AI to write your sprint goal or summarize a stakeholder update is a different category of use — and a less interesting one. The materials in this repo are built around a specific problem: **teams produce more data than any one person can hold in their head**. AI is useful here because it can read what you've already generated and tell you what you might have missed.

---

## Background

These tools were developed in active use as a Scrum Master supporting cross-functional teams. They grew out of a practical need: to move from reactive sprint reviews to proactive pattern recognition without adding ceremony overhead.

The approach was sharpened through the AI 4 Agile course by Stephan Wolpers, which provided a rigorous framework for where AI integration adds genuine value in agile contexts versus where it creates noise.
